02 — Philosophy

Good design leadership isn't about having the best taste in the room — it's about building the conditions where the right decisions become obvious. I've spent my career doing that at the seams: between hardware and software, between research and roadmap, between what a team can imagine and what it can actually ship.

03 — Experience

A route, not a résumé

  1. May 2025 — Jul 2026

    Director of User Experience

    Sonos · Seattle, WA

    Led multidisciplinary UX across hardware, setup, and system experiences for the Sonos portfolio — managing eight designers spanning hardware UX, app design, and creative technology. Reorganized around product outcomes with roughly 30% fewer people while holding delivery steady, and expanded live-system prototyping across three UX teams.

  2. Apr 2022 — May 2025

    Senior UX Manager

    Sonos · Seattle, WA

    Established hardware UX guidelines and system-level interaction principles that aligned industrial design, software, engineering, and product. Introduced rapid prototyping practices that cut early product-definition work from months to hours.

  3. Sept 2018 — Apr 2022

    UX Manager → Senior UX Manager

    Best Buy · Seattle, WA

    Led a design org of up to 15 direct reports and two UX managers. Doubled the team, expanded its reach from one product to three, and held 100% retention for two years. Built Best Buy's first digital membership platform team, contributing to a 2× increase in customer acquisition.

  4. Oct 2012 — Sep 2018

    Senior UX Designer → Design Director

    INRIX · Kirkland, WA

    Led a team of seven across automotive, mobile, and web. Directed UX for an in-car application ecosystem and a global traffic analytics platform, and integrated parking capabilities that grew parking-services revenue 8× in one year.

Earlier roles at Gripwire, Free & Clear, Pyramid Communications, and Snapvine — full history on the résumé. BFA Graphic Design, Miami University. HCI/UX Certificate, MIT CSAIL.
